      Fluency lab

      Calm tools for steadier speech: breath, paced reading, and gentle starts. Designed for people who stutter and for anyone who wants smoother flow.

      Inhale 4 · hold 1 · exhale 6 · hold 1. Three rounds settles the voice; six is a full warm-up.

        Accent shaping

        Targeted drills for Nigerian → American clarity. Keep your voice; sharpen the rhythm.

        In American English, content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives) carry the beat; small grammar words relax. Tap a sentence to hear it.

          Schwa (ə) is the most common American vowel. Unstressed syllables and small words reduce to it.

            American speech links words together — final consonants flow into next-word vowels. Read each phrase as one breath.

              Between vowels, American /t/ becomes a quick flap (almost a /d/). Tap to hear careful vs flapped.

                Power phrases for clear, confident speech. Use them to start a thought or to buy a calm second to think.
